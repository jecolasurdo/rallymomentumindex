Dave McGarvie, a volcanologist at Lancaster University, said: "It seems that after being relatively inactive for many centuries, this region is waking up.”The affected area is located near the town of Grindavík and the popular Blue Lagoon thousands of tourists visit ever year, as well as being nine miles away from Iceland's international airport.Although the volcano has not erupted in about 800 years there have been more recent eruptions offshore.Geological studies show the area is surrounded by five volcanic systems, which become active at the same time roughly every 1,000 years.
