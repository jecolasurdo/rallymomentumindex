Black Lives Matter protesters picket Black FridayHaley Dover, Free Press Staff Writer Published 1:58 p.m. ET Nov. 27, 2015 | Updated 3:19 p.m. ET Nov. 27, 2015Buy PhotoVicki Garrison leads a group of people in a Black Lives Matter Demonstration on Church Street on Friday.(Photo: HALEY DOVER/ FREE PRESS)Buy PhotoCONNECTTWEETLINKEDINCOMMENTEMAILMOREA group of protesters say they "won't shop until black lives matter.""We won't stop, we won't shop, we won't stop til black lives matter," Vicki Garrison chanted through a megaphone as a group of about 25 people followed behind her, repeating the words. Another pair held up a banner from the NAACP.Garrison, a native Vermonter, has been involved in numerous Black Lives Matter demonstrations during the past year.Children and families lined up on the marketplace to pet reindeer and to watch the noon parade. Others stood outside of City Hall waiting to catching a glimpse of Santa Claus' debut for the holiday season. As a group of people took the microphone to lead the audience in Christmas Carols, the protesters marched down Church Street shouting the names of police shooting victims."Jamar Clark, say his name," they chanted, referring to the black man who was fatally shot by Minneapolis police on Nov. 16.Buy PhotoAbout 25 people marched down Church Street on Friday chanting "we won't stop, we won't shop, we won't stop til black lives matter." (Photo: HALEY DOVER/ FREE PRESS)Ron Redmond, executive director of the Church Street Marketplace said the demonstration is part of the marketplace's culture."What the Black Lives Matter group is demonstrating for today is an issue that is of great importance all over the country," he said in a phone interview, adding that similar demonstrations are happening across the United States.Redmond said the marketplace serves as a community center for people to exercise their right under the First Amendment and a place where people can come to be together."This is what the street is all about," he said. "That people were here today protesting is just part of who we are, and it's important that people have a place to express themselves."FacebookTwitterGoogle+LinkedInBlack Lives Matter in Burlington FullscreenPost to FacebookPosted!A link has been posted to your Facebook feed.
