Cincinnati EnquirerCONNECTTWEETLINKEDINCOMMENTEMAILMOREProtests across Cincinnati on Wednesday were peaceful, with no arrests being made as large crowds gathered near the University of Cincinnati and Downtown. Cincinnati's sixth day of protests in response to the death of George Floyd in Minneapolis began on Clifton Avenue near the University of Cincinnati and was organized by college students.On Wednesday, Minnesota Attorney General Keith Ellison said that the charges for Derek Chauvin have been upgraded to second-degree murder. Ellison also said the three other officers at the scene when Floyd died last week have been charged with aiding and abetting murder, according to USA TODAY. Cincinnati EnquirerNo protest arrests made WednesdayA vigil held outside Cincinnati Police District One ended at 7:40 p.m., with the peaceful crowd dispersing. Protesters sang together outside of Police District One and drew with chalk on the street. Organizers encouraged members of the group to speak up, share poetry or sing during an open mic event.The Cincinnati Police Department said there were no arrests associated with the protests as of 11 p.m., when curfew went into effect. Cincinnati EnquirerPolice: Car driving into protesters 'not intentional'A car that drove into peaceful protesters Wednesday afternoon outside City Hall did not injure anyone, and police say they believe the incident was not intentional.The vehicle, a black BMW with Kentucky license plates, was registered in Fort Mitchell in September 2019, according to Carfax records.The driver has yet to be identified."If you were at this intersection and believe this vehicle intentionally struck you please file a police report," police said. The incident occurred just after the city council meeting ended at around 4 p.m. Wednesday, bringing to mind images from Charlottesville, Virginia, in 2017. The car rammed into protesters on Plum street near Cincinnati City Hall, where dozens of people stood in solidarity of the Black Lives Matter movement.Police at City Hall rushed at the car, which sped away before they could reach it. Cincinnati City Councilman P.G. Sittenfeld witnessed the incident as he was leaving the city council meeting. He said an active pursuit is taking place."Literally it felt like a Charlottesville-style murder attempt,” Sittenfeld said.Protests continue ThursdayOrganizers of Wednesday's vigil plan to be outside District 1 again Thursday. "The Vigil Against Police Violence will be a daily fixture of our Cincinnati resistance and is intended to be a space to: protest, stand together in solidarity, sing, read poetry, talk, share/teach protest chants+songs, pray, make signs for upcoming marches, cry together, say their names, and much more," the Facebook event states.Cincinnati Black United Front to host press conferenceThe Cincinnati Black United Front and the Ohio Justice & Policy Center will assemble for a press conference at City Hall on Thursday at 5:30 p.m. to present their path to justice."The first steps on that path will be the local action steps that the City of Cincinnati must take toward police accountability under the Collaborative Agreement and refresh," officials said.
