Updated: 11:05 PM PDT Sep 7, 2018 Walter Makaula Anchor A candlelight vigil was held Friday night where about 100 people gathered in Sacramento's Curtis Park neighborhood to draw attention to a deadly police shooting they believe was avoidable.The officer-involved shooting happened early Thursday morning. Police were responding to reports of a man with a mask and a gun. "I heard, 'pow, pow, pow, pow, pow, pow,'" said Candace Schunke, who lives in the neighborhood.She said just 15 minutes prior to the gunshots, she was awakened by some commotion outside."Someone was in my backyard with a flashlight going around," Schunke said.Sacramento SWAT officers were looking for an armed man who was spotted Wednesday night walking around 16th and Broadway while wearing a mask and carrying a handgun.A caller told a 911 dispatcher that the young man had pointed his gun at several people. Police said they found the man and ordered him to stop, but he ran off and into Schunke's neighborhood."(Police) said, 'Go inside. You know, you guys are better off inside,'" said George Avalos, who lives four houses away from the shooting scene. "They did a slow and methodical search clearing the yards," Sacramento police spokesperson Linda Matthew said.Police said he ran off into someone's yard and hopped over a fence. Nearly three-and-a-half hours later, SWAT officers found him hiding under a stairwell two doors away from Schunke's house.Police said when they told him to drop his weapon, he pointed his gun at the officers -- who then fired at him.Darell Richards, 19, died at the scene."The police keep putting out this narrative that they tried all the things they are supposed to try, that they followed policy by using non-lethal forms of force," said Tanya Faison of Black Lives Matter Sacramento. "But, if they sent their dog in there, their dog would have caught him because he was cornered.""Anytime we have a suspect who we are trying to take into custody, their actions determine what our actions are going to be," Mathew said.Officers recovered a knife and a pellet gun which was a replica of a Sig Sauer P225 9MM handgun.Police said there's several hours of footage that will corroborate their officers' stories. That footage will be released in about 30 days. The two Sacramento police officers involved are 11- and 17-year veterans of the department. Both have been placed on administrative leave, pending the outcome of an investigation. SACRAMENTO, Calif. Police were responding to reports of a man with a mask and a gun.
