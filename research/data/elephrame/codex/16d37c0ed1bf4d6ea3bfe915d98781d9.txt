More than 150 people took to Meadville's streets to protest deaths due to racisim KEITH GUSHARD/Meadville Tribune More than 150 people took to the streets of Meadville this afternoon in peaceful protest."We're protesting the deaths caused by racism — that's what we're protesting,” Jaida Speed, who organized today's march"There's a lot of love in Meadville,” Speed, 18, said of why she organized the event with her mother, Christina Smith. "We just want to get everybody together and make a change.” The crowd chanted "No justice, no peace” and "Black lives matter” as it walked from First District Elementary on North Main Street to a stop over in Diamond Park.At Diamond Park, protestors spent nearly nine minutes lying on down on the ground chanting "I can't breathe” in memory of George Floyd. Floyd died May 25 while in custody of a Minneapolis Police Department officer.Derek Chauvin, the police officer, has been charged with third-degree murder in connection with Floyd's death.Chauvin had his knee on Floyd's neck for nearly nine minutes — including almost three minutes while Floyd was unresponsive, according to court documents.The march then proceeded down South Main Street ending at Second District School at South Main and Linden streets.
