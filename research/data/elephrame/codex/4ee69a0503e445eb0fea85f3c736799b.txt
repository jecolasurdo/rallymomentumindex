Support Us buzzfeed.com Trending Protesters Take To The Streets In New York And Los Angeles Protesters in Los Angeles took to Beverly Boulevard for the Millions March LA. In New York, protesters called for justice for Akai Gurley. By Jim Dalrymple II Jim Dalrymple II BuzzFeed News Reporter Posted on December 27, 2014, at 8:30 p.m. ET Twitter Tweet Facebook Share Copy Copy Stephanie Keith / Reuters Protesters demanded justice for Akai Gurley in Brooklyn, New York City, Saturday. Protesters in New York City marched Saturday to demand justice for Akai Gurley, who was shot and killed by an NYPD officer on Nov. 20. About 200 people marched in Brooklyn, The New York Times reported. The march happened shortly after the funeral for Officer Rafael Ramos, one of the two officers killed in Brooklyn on Dec. 20. Gurley's family members were among those leading the march. View this vine on Vine vine.co View this vine on Vine vine.co In Los Angeles, thousands of protesters also marched Saturday in a demonstration against police violence. Millions March L.A. swelled to as many as 5,000 people, CBS Los Angeles reported. The demonstration began at Pan Pacific Park and progressed along centrally-located Beverly Boulevard. Participants chanted "hands up don't shoot" and "this is what democracy looks like." Shortly before dusk, the crowd began singing "we shall overcome."
