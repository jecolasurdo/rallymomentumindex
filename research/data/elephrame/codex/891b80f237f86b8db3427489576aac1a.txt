They urged its residents to give the "Black community the space to live" in the city. Shortly after, the crowd may its way down the water to meet the second group of protesters at Pier A.Allies4justice organizers lead a large crowd down Frank Sinatra Drive as they demand an end to racial injustice. (Photo: Jessie Gomez)Chants of "no justice, no peace" and "say her name" echoed throughout the waterfront as residents cheered from their high-rise apartments. Once at Pier A, a second group headed down 1st Street and onto Washington Street, a route organizers had not planned for. Hoboken resident Toni, who asked not to share her last name, said she was there along with her boyfriend to support the cause. For them, being there was a must. "How can you not be here?" she said. Hoboken Police stand on Washington Street as protesters make their way to Maxwell Place Park on Friday for a justice for George Floyd march. (Photo: Jessie Gomez)The swarm of protesters made their way onto Washington Street were they gathered outside of City Hall. A line of Hoboken Police Officers stood outside as protesters chanted "peaceful protest" and "hands up don't shoot." On Washington Street, dozens of businesses had already boarded up their windows as a precaution for looting or vandalism. Across from City Hall, Basile's Pizza had a large sign that read "United" and handed out free pizza and soda to demonstrators."This is our community and they deserve to be heard. We're supporting them,‚Äù Russell Basille said. On Tuesday, Hoboken Mayor Ravi Bhalla and Police Chief Ken Ferrante asked residents "to remain vigilant" during the protest and noted they were "taking every proactive measure possible to protect" the cty. Bhalla and his family joined Friday's march. "My family decided to join today because they believe it's not time to be bystanders and are committed to the cause of racial justice," Bhalla said on Twitter.
