Protesters close down Tower City, march to Euclid Avenue and E. 14th Street - cleveland.comSkip to ArticleMetroProtesters close down Tower City, march to Euclid Avenue and E. 14th StreetUpdated Jan 12, 2019; Posted Dec 20, 2014 Tamir Rice Protest at Public Square 18 Gallery: Tamir Rice Protest at Public Square By Adam Ferrise, cleveland.com CLEVELAND, Ohio — Protesters on Saturday temporarily caused Tower City to close during a protest that began at Public Square.Cleveland police officers and security guards blocked the main entrance to Tower City about 3:45 p.m.Cleveland Police Chief Calvin Williams addressed protesters who were complaining about the closure of Tower City."We've allowed you to do everything you've wanted to do," Williams told one protester.Some of the protesters tried insulting Williams and said they believed they would be attacked by officers for protesting."Has anyone in this city attacked you for protesting?" Williams asked. "That doesn't happen in this city."Protesters were angered that one woman with the group was caught inside after the doors were locked.Williams told one protester, Michael Hassell of Ferguson, Missouri, that if he convinced the demonstrators to back up, he'd allow the woman to rejoin the protests.Hassell told the protesters to move back. Williams then let the woman out and locked the doors.Several people who wanted to go inside Tower City to catch the RTA or shop were also barred from going inside.Protesters then marched down Euclid Avenue and stopped at the intersection of East 14th Street.Some protesters lay in the middle of the street.
