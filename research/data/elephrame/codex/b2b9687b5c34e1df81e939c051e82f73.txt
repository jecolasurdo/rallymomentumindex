Groups call for policy change after violent J-town arrestsAlso:Jeffersontown High principal steps down after school cop uses stun gun on studentBackground:Police officer uses stun gun on Jeffersontown student who assaulted cop, officials sayAt that point, other advocates rose and began calling out for action in the wake of last week's controversial arrests of two students at Jeffersontown. Board Chair Chris Brady then asked for security to remove the demonstrators before calling the meeting to a temporary adjournment.Demonstrators, including Chanelle Helm of Black Lives Matter Louisville, continued to call out their concerns. On their way out of the room, demonstrators chanted "Black lives matter!” and "Black kids matter!”The meeting then resumed, returning to the regularly scheduled agenda, while demonstrators' chants could still be heard outside the room.
