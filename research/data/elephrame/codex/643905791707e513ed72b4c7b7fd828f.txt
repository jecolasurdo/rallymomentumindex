'Black Lives Matter' protest blocks Saturday traffic in downtown Homewood - al.com Skip to ArticleBirmingham Real-Time News'Black Lives Matter' protest blocks Saturday traffic in downtown HomewoodUpdated Jan 13, 2019; Posted Feb 14, 2015 Black Lives Matter Rally in Homewood 18 Gallery: Black Lives Matter Rally in Homewood By Mia Watkins Shoppers and diners in downtown Homewood were met with message of social injustice as protesters gathered Saturday afternoon.The Black Lives Matter protest, one in a string of rallies in the last few months around the Birmingham metro area, blocked traffic between 29th Street South and 18th Avenue South for about two hours as protesters with banners and signs bearing the phrases such as "I Can't Breathe" chanted and marched. Homewood police attempted to redirect traffic while legal observers from the National Lawyers Guild looked on.No arrests were made during the seemingly peaceful protest, despite two warnings from officers for blocking traffic. A December demonstration saw two protesters arrested for criminal trespass in Homewood.Protesters declined to comment for this story.Previous protests include a gathering at the entrance of the Summit shopping center and a "die in" in the food court of Riverchase Galleria.Black Lives Matter was a movement organized nationwide as a response to the killings of Eric Garner in New York and Michael Brown in Ferguson by police.Note to readers: if you purchase something through one of our affiliate links we may earn a commission.DisclaimerRegistration on or use of this site constitutes acceptance of our User Agreement, Privacy Policy and Cookie Statement, and Your California Privacy Rights (each updated 1/1/20).Â© 2020 Advance Local Media LLC.
