The road was shut between Commercial Road and Roy Street and trams were diverted for about 45 minutes after protesters blocked the road at about 2pm.Organisers, the Whistleblowers, Activists and Citizens Alliance, said on Twitter that a group of about 100 people had picketed the United States consulate to show solidarity with the people of Ferguson, Missouri.Ferguson has been the scene of at-times violent protests after a grand jury there decided last week not to indict a white police officer who fatally shot unarmed 18-year-old Michael Brown.AdvertisementSome protesters were shown with placards reading "I can't breath", a reference to Eric Garner, who died after being placed in a chokehold by a New York police officer while being arrested on suspicion of selling untaxed cigarettes.A New York grand jury decided on Thursday not to charge the white police officer. St Kilda Road was reopened about 2.50pm.A Victoria Police spokeswoman said the protest was peaceful.
