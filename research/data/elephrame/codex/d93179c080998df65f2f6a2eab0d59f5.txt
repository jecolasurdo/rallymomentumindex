The school's administration was supportive, she said.The students planned to speak about unity and to have student poets perform.Some students held signs with the words "Black Lives Matter," in support of the national movement addressing racism."They believe in human rights," Roosevelt Principal Kevin Biggs said in a statement. "They believe in standing up when injustices occur, and in their minds, this is the best way to do it.
