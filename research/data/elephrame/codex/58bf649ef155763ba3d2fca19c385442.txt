Montebello turns out in protest of George Floyd's death Share this:Click to share on Facebook (Opens in new window)Click to share on Twitter (Opens in new window)Click to share on Reddit (Opens in new window)Click to email this to a friend (Opens in new window)Click to print (Opens in new window) More than 100 mostly young people are at City Park on. Whittier Boulevard in Montebello, California protesting the death of George Floyd on Thursday, June 4, 2020. (Staff photo by Mike Sprague) By Mike Sprague | msprague@scng.com | Whittier Daily NewsPUBLISHED: June 4, 2020 at 1:45 p.m. | UPDATED: June 4, 2020 at 3:22 p.m.Montebello was the site on Thursday, June 4 of the area's latest gathering to protest the in-custody death of George Floyd. More than 100 mostly young people initially came together at 1 p.m. at City Park on Whittier Boulevard to call for racial justice. Their numbers quickly doubled. They remained in place on the sidewalk. Among the chants was this one: "This is a peaceful protest.” Irene Garay, 22, of Montebello was one of the protest's organizers. She told those gathered: "We're have to get out of our comfort zone. We need to fight. If we don't do something, they'll kill all of us.” #MONTEBELLO: Irene Garay, 21, of Montebello, one of the organizers of a rally protesting the death of George Floyd, speaks. pic.twitter.com/YjZsnZ3uSo — Mike Sprague (@WhitReporter) June 4, 2020 As cars made their way down Whittier Boulevard, the drivers honked in approval. #MONTEBELLO: The protesters are getting support from drivers going by honking. pic.twitter.com/aeQ7vQyxzC — Mike Sprague (@WhitReporter) June 4, 2020 Isaac Becerra, 16, of Montebello, a Schurr High student. said he attended to protect "black lives and get rid of racism. We want to make our voices heard.” Similar protests have been held on Sunday and Tuesday in Whittier and on Wednesday in La Habra as well as across the San Gabriel Valley. Floyd died in Minneapolis on May 25 after a police officer held his knee to Floyd's neck for nearly 9 minutes. Before he lost consciousness, he was videotaped saying he couldn't breathe. The officer, Derek Chauvin, has been fired and charged with second-degree murder. Three other officers were also fired and have been charged with aiding and abetting murder. In Montebello, the protesters took knelt for 8 minutes, to represent the time George was pinned down by Chauvin's knee, which clocked in at just less than 9 minutes. Melissa Ramirez, 21, of Montebello, said those present wanted to show their solidarity with Black Lives Matter. "We're here to continue the movement. We're here to represent the voices of the people who are unheard,” she said.
