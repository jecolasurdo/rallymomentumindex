Updated: 7:49 PM PST Mar 6, 2019 KCRA Staff A protest outside of the Sacramento Police Department spilled onto the roadway Wednesday evening. The small group marched on Freeport Boulevard for about an hour before returning to police headquarters. Capt. Norm Leong tweeted officers got into place to offer traffic breaks.The protest, organized by Black Lives Matter-Sacramento, is calling for justice in Stephon Clark's police shooting death. They want the officers involved in the shooting to be fired.The march comes a day after the California Attorney General's Office announced it will not be filing criminal charges against the officers who shot and killed Clark last year. On Saturday, the Sacramento County District Attorney's Office also announced it will not be filing charges against the officers.The protest began as a sit-in at the Sacramento police headquarters around 3 p.m. Around 5:30 p.m., protesters began marching down Freeport Boulevard.The protesters held signs and chanted as they walked north, toward Broadway.The march stop near Sacramento City College, where protesters stood in the roadway and chanted for sometime.Around 6:15 p.m., the group turned around and began marching southbound on Freeport Boulevard.By 6:30 p.m., the protesters were crossing Irvin Way.The group reached police headquarters by 6:45 p.m., where they continued to protest for another 40 minutes.The protest dispersed by 7:30 p.m. SACRAMENTO, Calif.
