Updated: 4:19 PM EDT Apr 11, 2015 A Greater Cincinnati organization spoke out on police violence.The Cincinnati chapter of Black Lives Matter held a rally for justice at Bond Hill Park Saturday.The rally was organized by Black Lives Cincinnati and was inspired by the most recent shooting death of an unarmed black man in South Carolina.However, according to the organizers, the intended message was to affect social justice denied to all people."The only way that this is going to change is through our pressure," Black Lives Cincinnati spokesman Brian Taylor said. "Getting out and raising our voices, having something to say and coming up with actual demands.""It's a complex problem, not just of police brutality but institutional racism and economic injustice. And I think it's important to put together those movements," Oakley resident Thurman Wenzel said.Organizers say their numbers were small Saturday, but their message will be heard loud, and clear by leaders across the city and nationwide.Officials said Black Lives Cincinnati is planning another rally for Washington Park in May.
