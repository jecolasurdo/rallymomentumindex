(Kendall Rodriguez/for New York Daily News) The mother of Eric Garner says the recent police killings of black men in Louisiana and Minnesota transported her instantly back to 2014 and the death of her son. "When I heard about it early that morning, I decided not to watch it," said Gwen Carr during a Saturday march against police brutality. "It was like my son getting killed all over again. It just reduced me to tears." Advertisement Carr was joined by the mothers of Amadou Diallo and other black New Yorkers killed by the NYPD for a Brooklyn demonstration held one day before the second anniversary of Garner's death. His final words — "I can't breathe" — became a call for action across the last 24 months. Advertisement The mothers of Eric Garner, Amadou Diallo and other black New Yorkers gunned down by the NYPD march in Brooklyn with the Rev Al. Sharpton. (Kendall Rodriguez/for New York Daily News) Carr and her family remain hopeful that Officer Daniel Pantaleo, cleared of criminal charges in Garner's death, will still face federal civil rights charges in the fatal arrest. About 200 people, including the Rev. Al Sharpton, members of his National Action Network, and Black Lives Matter protesters marched through the borough to Prospect Park. Carr, whose son died in a police chokehold during his Staten Island arrest on July 17, 2014, called for peace between police and the black community. Al Sharpton consoles Esaw Garner on the eve of the two-year anniversary of her husband's chokehold death amid an arrest by NYPD cops on Staten Island. (Stephanie Keith/Getty Images) "You shouldn't go around shooting innocent police," said Carr, referencing the ambush that left five Dallas police officers dead. "Those police didn't kill your loved ones. We need unity among all people no matter who they are."
