Author: FOX43 Newsroom Published: 11:11 PM EDT May 30, 2020 Updated: 12:40 PM EDT May 31, 2020 LANCASTER COUNTY, Pa. — Hundreds of people in Lancaster protested on Saturday morning in response to the death of George Floyd. Organizers called the protest "We've had enough, time to stand." The protest began at 11 a.m. and went on for several hours. "As a filmmaker I know how valuable a story can be," Amir Marquise, protest organizer said. "So, my goal was to get people to gather and tell stories and show their frustration so people can get a clear view of how it's affecting their neighbors and friends." The protest remained peaceful, Marquise said.
