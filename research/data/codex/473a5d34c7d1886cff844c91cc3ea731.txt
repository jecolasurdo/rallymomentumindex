Auburn athletes, coaches attend Black Lives Matter protest at Toomer's CornerJosh Vitale, Montgomery Advertiser Published 7:30 p.m. CT June 7, 2020 | Updated 7:55 p.m. CT June 7, 2020CLOSE Auburn coaches and athletes were among those who marched to Toomer's Corner on Sunday, June 7, 2020. Montgomery AdvertiserCONNECTTWEETLINKEDINCOMMENTEMAILMOREAUBURN — The original plan, described by head coach Gus Malzahn this past Tuesday, was for Auburn's football players to be isolated together for the first week they were back on campus due to concern over COVID-19.But some things are more important than football. That's why Malzahn, wife Kristi, many of his assistant coaches and a host of players — at least more than half the roster — attended the Black Lives Matter protest that took place Sunday evening in downtown Auburn.Gus Malzahn leads the Auburn football team to the Black Lives Matter rally at Toomer's Corner on Sunday. pic.twitter.com/a8TPNNoJPU— Auburn Gold Mine (@AUGoldMine) June 7, 2020The protest, organized by Auburn Students and Community for Change, included a march from Toomer's Corner to the Auburn Police Department headquarters and back, as well as a series of speeches, the reading of a poem and the singing of "Amazing Grace" right on the tiger paw at the intersection of College Street and Magnolia Ave.MORE: Auburn athletics speaks out against racial injusticeThe message: "Enough is enough," after the police killings of Breonna Taylor in Louisville, Kentucky, George Floyd in Minneapolis, and too many others. That black lives matter. That it's time to listen, condemn racism and hold people accountable. MORE: Amid protests, Greene, Malzahn engage Auburn in 'difficult' conversationsSenior linebacker Chandler Wooten held a sign that read "Don't support us on the field and kill us off the (field)."#BlackLivesMatterpic.twitter.com/jIFYAceq64— CWoot (@ItsMeWootang) June 8, 2020Among the other Auburn athletics representatives in attendance were athletics director Allen Greene and his family; men's basketball coach Bruce Pearl and his staff (including director of basketball operations Chad Prewett and daughter Madison Prewett, who was a contestant on the most recent season of The Bachelor); and baseball coach Butch Thompson.Police blocked off car traffic through Toomer's Corner, but otherwise did not make their presence known.
