Updated: 7:14 PM PDT Apr 28, 2017 KCRA Staff Dozens of people are refusing to leave the Sacramento City Hall area as they protest police officers receiving medals in the killing of a homeless man. Sacramento police said about six protesters are inside City Hall participating in a sit-in, along with a larger group of protesters outside. Officers are talking with the protesters and continue to give them opportunities to leave the building. Police said if they continue to stay inside the building, they can face arrest for trespassing since City Hall closed at 5 p.m. Police said the protest is peaceful. The protest was put on by the Black Lives Matter Sacramento rapid-response team. Black Lives Matter officials said they wanted to protest the death of Dazion Flenaugh.
