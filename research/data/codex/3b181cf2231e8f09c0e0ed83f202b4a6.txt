Sitting or kneeling during the anthem has become a standard method of protesting racial injustice since 2016, following the example of football player Colin Kaepernick.Breaking news, at your fingertips. Expulsion! When do we want it? Now!" they yelled at halftime."Which side are you on, UT? Become a digital subscriber.Vice Chancellor for Student Life Vince Carilli said Monday afternoon, at a meeting with students and UT officials, that the university was still deciding how to discipline students who appeared in blackface in a Snapchat image last week. He said it was unlikely they would be expelled. Calls to Carilli on Wednesday were directed to UT director of media relations, Tyra Haag. Haag, also contacted Tuesday evening for comment, did not return calls.In the image, two people appear to wear black skincare masks."We for racial equality boys," the caption read. "Bout to get this free college now that I'm black let's gooooo #blacklivesmatter." All four people visible in the image are believed to be UT students.The university quickly condemned the photo, but at Monday's meeting Carilli said those involved were unlikely to be expelled for "expressing their First Amendment rights.”That didn't sit well with many students at the meeting.Campus officials agreed more work needs to be done to promote diversity and inclusion.
