Updated: 6:53 PM EDT Jul 25, 2016 WMTW News 8 Protesters came together Monday for a monthly Black Lives Matter vigil along Longley Bridge.It's organized by the Central Maine Showing Up for Racial Justice group based in Auburn.Central Maine SURJ is the local chapter of a national organization.A few dozen people attended this demonstration.The vigils, which happen the fourth Monday of every month, are designed to raise awareness for the Black Lives Matter campaign in a peaceful way.The events are considered family-friendly.Activists said attendance at Monday's gathering was double what it has been in the past.For Wendy Schlotterbeck, recent racial violence is especially troubling."It's unjust. It's devastating to hear. I mean, it's just, day after day after day,” she said.Several counter-protesters showed up at the demonstration.Auburn Mayor Jonathan LaBonte was also there and said he wanted to check out the scene.Auburn and Lewiston police were not at Monday's vigil.Get the WMTW App AUBURN, Maine — Protesters came together Monday for a monthly Black Lives Matter vigil along Longley Bridge.It's organized by the Central Maine Showing Up for Racial Justice group based in Auburn.
