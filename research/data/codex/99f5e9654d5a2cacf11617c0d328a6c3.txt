No arrests in New Brunswick police brutality protest - nj.comSkip to ArticleMiddlesexNo arrests in New Brunswick police brutality protestUpdated Mar 29, 2019; Posted Dec 06, 2014 By Brian Amaral | NJ Advance Media for NJ.com NEW BRUNSWICK — Police made no arrests in last night's march and demonstration against police brutality, a city spokeswoman said.Demonstrators gathered Friday night and marched from the Douglass Student Center to various parts of the city, at one point blocking traffic on Route 18 and the bridge from Highland Park to New Brunswick, according to social media reports.The protests led to traffic delays, but no other major incidents, according to spokeswoman Jennifer Bradshaw. The city also canceled its holiday tree-lighting ceremony in response to the protests.At one point, demonstrators held a "die-in" at the New Brunswick Police Department, lying on the ground in front of the building.Christopher Paladino, the president of the New Brunswick Development Corporation, wrote on Twitter of the police protest: "Very orderly and enthusiastic."Die -In at the NBPD this evening led by Rutgers Students, very orderly and enthusiastic #Rutgers #newbrunswick pic.twitter.com/cOkMyM1lP6— Christopher Paladino (@DEVCONB) December 6, 2014 The protest was held in response to the decisions by grand juries in Missouri and New York not to indict police officers who had killed unarmed black men.New Brunswick itself has been a hotbed of protest: Barry Deloatch, an unarmed black man, was shot and killed by police in September 2011.
