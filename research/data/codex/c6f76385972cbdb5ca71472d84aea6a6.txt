Updated: 2:55 PM CDT May 30, 2020 Quanecia Fraser Reporter Protesters gathered Friday to respond to the death of George Floyd in Minneapolis police custody.By Friday night, smoke and gas filled the air around 72nd and Dodge Streets. Hours before tear gas and pepper balls were deployed, the crowds were by the sidewalks for the peaceful protest that started around 6 p.m."White, brown, purple, we all care and we're all coming together. That's a beautiful thing," Cortez Washington said. By 8 p.m., demonstrators moved into the street. Officers from different law enforcement agencies responded, trying to disperse the crowd.Omaha Police said they arrested at least 21 people.By 1 a.m. Saturday, the area was mostly clear. But 20-year-old Tyler McDonough said he was still determined to show his support."But when riots get out of control, it's pain and agony. And it's so much because there's hundreds of years of pent up rage," he told KETV Newswatch 7.Councilman Ben Gray echoed a similar perspective hours before the chaos erupted."I don't care for the violence, but I understand the violence," Gray said.Like many, he wants peace but he wants change too."This stuff cannot continue to go on. If it does, and if this gets worse, you're going to watch more cities burn," Gray said.Washington said, "Honestly, for change to happen, I think we should all come together as one — the police, the community."
