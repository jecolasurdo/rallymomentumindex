Updated: 6:30 PM EDT Aug 30, 2014 Ben Petracco Digital Editor About 200 people gathered in protest Saturday outside a Beavercreek Walmart where a Fairfield man was fatally shot by police.Organizers said the rally was the latest efforts by people wanting to shed more light on the Aug. 5 shooting death of John Crawford III inside the store.Authorities said police responded after a caller said a man was waving a gun. Police said Crawford refused orders to put down the air rifle he was holding."We stand with this Crawford family here today. We stand in solidarity saying Ohio attorney general, something is not right. Something stinks and we want justice and we want it now," said president of the Greater Cincinnati National Action Network Bishop Bobby Hilton.Meanwhile, an Ohio state official continues to push for the actual store surveillance video from inside the Walmart to be released.State Rep. Alicia Reece is now speaking out saying that the release of the video will give more transparency to the investigation."Let's have transparency, let's release all of the tapes so we know what the truth is," Reece said.The Ohio Attorney General's Office is investigating the shooting. BEAVERCREEK, Ohio — About 200 people gathered in protest Saturday outside a Beavercreek Walmart where a Fairfield man was fatally shot by police.Organizers said the rally was the latest efforts by people wanting to shed more light on the Aug. 5 shooting death of John Crawford III inside the store.
