"We definitely are recognizing what happened in Dallas. We are recognizing what happened in Baton Rouge. We are recognizing all of it."Police Chief Michael Sauschuck was at the protest, as were about a dozen other officers.Protest organizers said they met with police for several hours Friday."We're not an issue-driven profession," Sauschuck said. "We're here to protect and facilitate somebody's First Amendment rights. It could be any cause. We have been in this square many, many times."Following a 30-minute ceremony, police escorted marchers down Congress Street. They headed to City Hall and then the Portland police station.Get the WMTW App9234908 PORTLAND, Maine — Hundreds of people gathered in Monument Square, and then marched through downtown shouting "Black lives matter!" as part of a peaceful protest against violence.The group, Kesho Wazo, which means "tomorrow's ideas" in Swahili, had planned the event after two shootings involving white police officers and black men.
