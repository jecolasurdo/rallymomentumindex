Updated: 12:30 PM CDT Jun 5, 2020 KETV Staff Report About 300 UNMC and Nebraska Medicine colleagues and students took part in a national effort called White Coats 4 Black Lives at noon on Friday.The group took a knee in silence outside the Fred & Pamela Buffett Cancer Center for 10 minutes in "recognition of the death of George Floyd and continued racial injustice."Helen Grace, D.O., an assistant professor in pediatrics, said, "It's important for us to stand up and say to the black community, we see you, we hear you, we know the discrimination is systemic, and we want to make it better. We are committed to change."Another group gathered at 18th and Harney streets. The group of women said they want better for their children. "For the first time it feels like everybody sees this story, it used to be only the black community saw this story," said Monica Chatmon, with Women of Omaha Unite For Change.
