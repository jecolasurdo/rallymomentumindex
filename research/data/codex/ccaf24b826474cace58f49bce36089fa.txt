View Full Caption DNAinfo/Danielle Tcholakian MIDTOWN — Nine people were arrested during a Black Lives Matter protest in Midtown on Wednesday night, police said. Demonstrators marched from Union Square to Herald Square in solidarity with protesters in Charlotte, North Carolina, where massive unrest has taken place after the shooting of Keith Lamont Scott by officers. #BlackLivesMatter march now occupying Macy's Herald Square in NYC#KeithLamontScott#NYC2Charlotte pic.twitter.com/7dXuo0Dzkb — JamesFromTheInternet (@JamesFTInternet) September 22, 2016 Eight protesters were charged with disorderly conduct and one with reckless endangerment, an NYPD spokesman said. North Carolina Gov. Pat McCrory declared a state of emergency Wednesday night for Charlotte after a protester was shot and critically wounded, WNBC reported.
