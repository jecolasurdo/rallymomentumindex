Updated: 11:43 PM EST Feb 28, 2016 Dozens of protesters with Black Lives Matter Cincinnati marched to the Cincinnati District 3 Police Headquarters on Sunday.The group demanded justice for Paul Gaston and Melvin Murray Jr., two men killed in confrontations with Cincinnati police officers in District 3.Watch this storyGaston, 27, was shot dead by police on Feb. 17. Gaston was shot multiple times in the 3300 block of Harrison Avenue. Police said he reached for a gun in his waistband, disregarding their commands and putting their lives in danger.Police said the realistic-looking weapon he reached for turned out to be an Airsoft pellet gun.Black Live Matter Cincinnati claims that "his murder is testament to the double standard that black men face when dealing with police in the US.” The group references an event in Mt. Healthy days prior, in which a white man pointed a fake gun at officers, but was taken into custody.Black Lives Matter Cincinnati demanded that the officers involved in the shooting death of Gaston be fired and indicted for murder.The group also asked the department to reopen the death investigation of Melvin Murray Jr. last September.Murray was killed in a single vehicle accident on Sept. 4. Police said they saw Murray make an improper turn. An officer witnesses the turn, and attempted to catch the vehicle, officers said.Cincinnati police said the officer thought Murray was "trying to evade him” and gave chase. Murray lost control of his car, crashed into a tree and was pronounced dead at the scene, officers said.Murray's mother said police did not help while her son was dying in his car."Why didn't you aid him? Why didn't you help him? Answer that Cincinnati police,” Robyn Scott said."Until there's justice for everybody and until the police (are) held accountable, we need to stand up and speak out," protester Alessandra Trindle said.Officials maintain that Gaston's shooting death was justified. CINCINNATI — Dozens of protesters with Black Lives Matter Cincinnati marched to the Cincinnati District 3 Police Headquarters on Sunday.The group demanded justice for Paul Gaston and Melvin Murray Jr., two men killed in confrontations with Cincinnati police officers in District 3.
