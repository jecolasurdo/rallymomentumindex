Updated: 9:33 PM EDT Jul 23, 2016 Mike Cronin News Reporter Protesters took to the streets for a Black Lives Matter rally Saturday afternoon in Manchester.View the report.The march started and ended at Veterans Park. It was the second Black Lives Matter rally in Manchester in a week.The first event drew hundreds but Saturday about two dozen came out.Protesters walked down the sidewalks of Elm St. in a peaceful gathering. The road was open to traffic.Organizers said they want to continue to spread awareness about their cause.They were joined by police officers who walked with them for protection.Behind them was about half-dozen people armed with guns. They were there to say all lives matter and to stand by the messages they believe in."We're here to support the community. We're here to support the police force. We're here to support those guys because we're doing what we believe in which is freedom, liberty and justice for all," a man standing guard named Dean said.A march organizer, Peya Urena, said, "I want to spread awareness. I want people to realize that if we're on the streets, sweating, basically melting for this, that we have a cause. I want people to notice."Black Lives Matter organizers said they are hoping to hold more rallies in the Granite State.
