Group holds downtown Phoenix vigil in memory of Stephon ClarkVictor Ren, The Republic | azcentral.com Published 9:00 p.m. MT March 30, 2018 | Updated 9:20 p.m. MT March 30, 2018CLOSE Results of an independent autopsy performed on Stephon Clark by Dr. Bennet Omalu explains how bullets penetrate Clark body during the press conference at the South Sacramento Christian Center. A group of about 20 kneel and pray during a vigil for Stephon Clark, who was shot and killed by Sacramento police.(Photo: Victor Ren)CONNECTTWEETLINKEDINCOMMENTEMAILMOREA small group of protesters attempted to shut down Central Avenue in downtown Phoenix Friday night during a rally for Stephon Clark.Sacramento police shot and killed Clark, 22, in his grandmother's backyard. He was unarmed.About 20 people attended the Good Friday Justice Rally and Martyrdom Vigil outside Phoenix City Hall. According to organizer Jarrett Maupin, the intent was to raise awareness about excessive force during incidents involving black men and women. He said the focus of the event was "prayerful action." Organizers said they planned to try to shut down the streets in the area. But shortly after they began to march in the street, police arrived and the group peacefully moved to the sidewalks. At one point, the group knelt down to pray. It doesn't matter what someone was doing," she said. "People who do such killings should be held accountable."Arielle Ruben was among those at the vigil. "The problem is not getting better," she said. "Shot in the back eight times ... all the things that do not make sense. I can't imagine what the black community is feeling right now, what Stephon Clark's family is feeling right now."
