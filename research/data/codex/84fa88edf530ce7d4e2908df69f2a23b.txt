Updated: 11:01 PM CDT May 31, 2020 Emma Claybrook Multimedia Journalist Hundreds of people peacefully protested the death of George Floyd in Fort Smith Sunday. Protesters lined Rogers Ave. for more than five hours. They held signs and chanted things like "I can't breathe" and "Black lives matter" as cars honked in support.Isaac Haynes, a protester from Fort Smith, said it was important to him that the protest stay peaceful."This is love as I am watching it right now. It is truly one of the most amazing sights I have ever seen in my life. People are gathering for the culture. Not just for the black culture, but for everybody's culture," Haynes said. Leiloni Jackson said she came to the protest to show her children that it is important to speak up. "It was important for me to come out here because I am raising young black men and women for the future. We want the injustice to stop. We are tired of being treated unequally," Jackson said. Israel Jackson lives in Van Buren, but came to the protest in Fort Smith to show her support. "I think a huge part of being part of a community and part of a nation is acknowledging that, naturally, we were born with some privilege, but then using that to help and make a difference and speak up. I think we wouldn't be doing our part if we didn't come down here," Jackson said. A few law enforcement officials monitored the protest to make sure protesters stayed safe. A few protesters did walk out into the road to try to block traffic, but they quickly returned to the sidewalk to protest. FORT SMITH, Ark. — Hundreds of people peacefully protested the death of George Floyd in Fort Smith Sunday. Protesters lined Rogers Ave. for more than five hours. They held signs and chanted things like "I can't breathe" and "Black lives matter" as cars honked in support.
