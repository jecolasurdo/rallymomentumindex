Looters pried open the security gate, broke the window and cleared out the Super Discount Pharmacy on the 14600 block of Victory Boulevard. A number of similar businesses had also been targeted by thieves in search of codeine and other prescription narcotics, Bert said. Media helicopters followed looters as they snaked through Van Nuys alleys, dropping of stolen goods in their vehicles and returning for more. Late Monday afternoon, police spotted a group of suspected looters in a dark-colored Dodge Charger, prompting a police pursuit. Some suspects were seen bailing out of that car during the chase, but the pursuit was continuing as the driver circled area streets. In the early evening, looters were seen on Twitter video hitting the Don Larry Watch & Jewelry shop on Hamlin Street and Kovac's Care Pharmacy on nearby Gilmore Street. The looting continued north along Van Nuys Boulevard, where victimized businesses included the Walgreens drug store and Big 5 Sporting Goods near Sherman Way, Bert said. #LAPD takes suspects into custody at #Walgreens at Van Nuys and Sherman Way Monday afternoon. The night before, looters hit a jewelry store and CVS drug store on the 17300 block of Ventura Boulevard in the Encino area, breaking windows and taking what they could at both businesses. Nearly 1,200 people were reportedly arrested Sunday across Los Angeles County as protesters continued clashing with police and National Guard troops. In Santa Monica and Long Beach, looters shattered windows and emptied stores largely unimpeded by law enforcement despite a 6 p.m. curfew. The word of a protest in Van Nuys prompted concern from residents ‚Äî and particularly business owners who feared they would not financially survive a looting incident after being shuttered for two months due to the coronavirus pandemic. Hundreds of additional officers will be deployed in to augment police in the valley in the wake of the looting that happened in Van Nuys on Monday, and across Southern California over the past several days, Bert said. Before the looting broke out, about a dozen local business owners and other community members kept an eye on the ongoing protest in hopes that it wouldn't devolve into lawless violence like in other LA communities.
