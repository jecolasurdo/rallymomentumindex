Updated: 11:03 PM EDT May 29, 2020 Hundreds of people marched the streets of Portland on Friday to protest the death of Minnesota man George Floyd.On Friday, the fired Minneapolis police officer seen kneeling on Floyd's neck before his death was charged with murder.The Maine protest started in Monument Square before the protesters marched to Portland police headquarters.They chanted and carried signs that said phrases like "black lives matter” and "I can't breathe.”The protesters also gathered on a section of Franklin Street that was shut down near I-295.Throughout the protest, police said there were no serious issues.One protester said standing in solidarity with so many people was powerful."It meant so much because a majority of the people here aren't even of color and they're really thinking about what's happening to the communities,” protester Kristina Kirilova said.After marching to the intersection of Franklin Street and Marginal Way, the protesters marched to Congress Square Park where a small crowd continued to protest until about 7:30 p.m.Most protesters wore masks, but social distancing was not widely observed.
