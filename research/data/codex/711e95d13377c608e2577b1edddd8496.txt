(Steve Schering, Pioneer Press) A large and vocal crowd was on hand as the Oak Park Village Board passed a "black lives matter" proclamation at its June 29 meeting. Just days after the shooting that took nine lives at Emanuel African Methodist Episcopal Church in Charleston, S.C., Village President Anan Abu-Taleb read the proclamation, which was passed unanimously by trustees. Advertisement The proclamation noted how African-Americans have contributed to the history of Oak Park and offered the village's condolences to those affected by the shooting in South Carolina. "One of the things that I know I am particularly proud of and a reason I came to Oak Park is because of diversity," Trustee Peter Barber said. "We don't have this kind of culture and a mix by accident."
