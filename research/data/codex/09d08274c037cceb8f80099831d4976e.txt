He was fatally shot Oct. 22 by three sheriff's deputies following a car chase on Cypress Avenue."He exited his car with a Tec 22 firearm," Sacramento County Sheriff's Sgt. Tony Turnbull said.He said the weapon had "a high capacity magazine, which had 30 rounds in it, and leveled it at the officers from behind the car. That's when the shots were fired at the suspect."Ludd's mother, Naomi LaTrisha Ludd-Randle, said during a news conference Monday, "We don't know," if there was any evidence to dispute the sheriff's depiction of events.At that point, Faison said, "That's enough questions."Authorities said Ludd was also armed with a second weapon, a 25-caliber semi-automatic handgun, when he was killed in a hail of gunfire by sheriff's deputies.Ludd's supporters are demanding to see the evidence in the investigation into the officer-involved shooting."Since the shooting on Oct. 22, the family of Adrien Ludd has not obtained a police report or existing dash cam footage of the three police vehicles that were at the scene," Faison said.Organizers from Black Lives Matter contend that law enforcement is hiding evidence in this case, but the sheriff's department insists it is following protocol by not releasing the dash-cam video."It's not just this case," Turnbull said. "We do this with many other investigation cases, we do with a lot of sexual assault cases, it's one of those things that it's really important to preserve that integrity."The sheriff's department has until April 2019 to release the video, but members of Monday's protest said they want greater transparency now.Under California code, authorities have until April 2019 to complete a thorough investigation.Jeanelle Hope said that she is asking, "for better community relations, as well as to be be in better communication with the family so we can know exactly what happened, so we can continue a thorough investigation."Black Lives Matter Sacramento is also demanding an independent review of the incident by Inspector General Rick Braziel.Braziel said in an email, "The Office of Inspector General has started an independent review of the events and actions resulting in the death of Adriene Ludd. The review will include but is not limited to:"- Crime reports and physical evidence related to the death of Adriene Lund"- Department police and manuals"- Video recordings"- Site visits to critical locations and the pursuit route"- Interviews of witnesses." SACRAMENTO, Calif. (KCRA) — Black Lives Matter protesters demanded answers from Sacramento County Sheriff Scott Jones on Monday after deputies shot and killed an armed Carmichael man in October."Adriene Ludd was a 36-year-old black man killed by Sacramento sheriff's deputies," Black Lives Matter spokesperson Tanya Faison said. Tony Turnbull said.He said the weapon had "a high capacity magazine, which had 30 rounds in it, and leveled it at the officers from behind the car. That's when the shots were fired at the suspect."Ludd's mother, Naomi LaTrisha Ludd-Randle, said during a news conference Monday, "We don't know," if there was any evidence to dispute the sheriff's depiction of events.At that point, Faison said, "That's enough questions."Authorities said Ludd was also armed with a second weapon, a 25-caliber semi-automatic handgun, when he was killed in a hail of gunfire by sheriff's deputies.Ludd's supporters are demanding to see the evidence in the investigation into the officer-involved shooting."Since the shooting on Oct. 22, the family of Adrien Ludd has not obtained a police report or existing dash cam footage of the three police vehicles that were at the scene," Faison said.Organizers from Black Lives Matter contend that law enforcement is hiding evidence in this case, but the sheriff's department insists it is following protocol by not releasing the dash-cam video."It's not just this case," Turnbull said. "We do this with many other investigation cases, we do with a lot of sexual assault cases, it's one of those things that it's really important to preserve that integrity."The sheriff's department has until April 2019 to release the video, but members of Monday's protest said they want greater transparency now.Under California code, authorities have until April 2019 to complete a thorough investigation.Jeanelle Hope said that she is asking, "for better community relations, as well as to be be in better communication with the family so we can know exactly what happened, so we can continue a thorough investigation."Black Lives Matter Sacramento is also demanding an independent review of the incident by Inspector General Rick Braziel.Braziel said in an email, "The Office of Inspector General has started an independent review of the events and actions resulting in the death of Adriene Ludd.
