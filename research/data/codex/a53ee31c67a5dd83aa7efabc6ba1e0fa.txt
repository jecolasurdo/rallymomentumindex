Updated: 10:51 PM EDT Jun 4, 2020 Mike Cronin News Reporter Peaceful protests were held Thursday across New Hampshire to remember George Floyd, who was killed while in police custody in Minneapolis.>> Download the free WMUR appOrganizers of a march in Portsmouth held on Thursday evening said they worked with the city and police to make sure that it's a safe event for everyone. People were encouraged to wear face coverings and social distance as best they can.In the heart of the city, the group came together to hold signs, chant and be the voice for change. The large crowd flooded downtown Portsmouth. Hundreds marched with a unified message to fight racism in New Hampshire and across the county."For me, it's really important to come out because clearly, I'm half black and just the events that have been happening the past few months have been really angering," said Shaylyn Saunders of Hudson. "I just really want to see a change in the country."With police standing guard throughout Market Square, the protest was peaceful. They said the event was about bringing attention to racism now and until there's sustainable change in America.Four former Minneapolis police officers have been charged in connection with Floyd's death."It's great that they've been charged, but we need to see them be convicted and then from there, we need to see their sentences be appropriate, the maximum that would be allowed under Minnesota law," said organizer Ramsay Dean.Events were also held Thursday in Exeter and Salem.
