Courtesy of Lilly Forsyth Facebook Twitter WhatsApp SMS Email Print Save The usually bustling Nittany Lion Shrine was silent this Blue and White weekend as over a dozen individuals stood at the photographic location with signs and requests.Roughly 10 individuals laid at the foot of the statue, similar to protests after the 2014 killing of Michael Brown.One man stood at the front of the gathering, handing out slips of paper to pedestrians. The statements were related to last month's shooting of 29-year-old State College resident Osaze Osagie.Controversy surrounded his death due to the circumstances of his death. As previously reported by the Daily Collegian, Osagie's father asked police officers to check in on his son, who had a history of mental health-related problems.Upon arrival, the police reported Osagie approached them with a knife, prompting one officer to open fire, ultimately killing Osagie.The paper being handed out included the following:Fire and charge the officers involvedCommunity oversight of investigation (commission, task force, committee)People of color in the community to provide checks and balancesPSU students, trusted community figuresTransparency of eventOption for family to review body-cam footageFunds and resources provided to family for grief and counseling expensesThe individuals were not open to questions.RELATED One dead, officers on administrative leave following Wednesday shooting UPDATE, April 30 'A kind, gentle, simple young man': Friends remember the life of Osaze Osagie David Adewumi grew up with Osaze Osagie in State College.
